PROJECT PROPOSAL: Employee Wellness Platform
Submitted by: Human Resources Department
Date: July 2, 2026
Version: 1.2

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This proposal outlines the development and implementation of a comprehensive Employee Wellness Platform for Aurora Innovations. The platform aims to address declining employee satisfaction scores, which have dropped from 82% to 67% over the past eighteen months, and rising healthcare costs that have increased by approximately 15% annually. The proposed solution integrates physical health tracking, mental health resources, financial wellness tools, and social connection features into a single, accessible platform.

2. PROBLEM STATEMENT

Employee burnout and stress levels have reached critical levels across the organization. Our annual employee survey revealed that 43% of employees report feeling burned out, 38% cite work-life balance as a major concern, and 52% say they would benefit from better access to mental health resources. Additionally, absenteeism due to stress-related illnesses has increased by 22% compared to last year. The current piecemeal approach of offering separate benefits through different vendors is confusing and underutilized, with only 12% of employees actively using available wellness resources.

3. PROPOSED SOLUTION

The Employee Wellness Platform will be a web and mobile application with the following modules:

a) Physical Wellness Module
   - Integration with fitness trackers (Apple Watch, Fitbit, Garmin)
   - Step challenges and team competitions
   - Gym reimbursement tracking
   - Ergonomic workstation assessment tool

b) Mental Health Module
   - Access to licensed therapists via video sessions
   - Guided meditation and mindfulness exercises
   - Stress assessment and personalized recommendations
   - Anonymous peer support groups

c) Financial Wellness Module
   - Budgeting tools and financial planning resources
   - 401(k) and retirement planning guidance
   - Student loan management assistance
   - Financial health score tracking

d) Social Connection Module
   - Interest-based employee groups and clubs
   - Company-wide events calendar
   - Recognition and kudos system
   - Mentorship matching program

4. IMPLEMENTATION PLAN

Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Requirements gathering, vendor selection, and prototype development
Phase 2 (Months 4-6): Core feature development and integration with existing HR systems
Phase 3 (Months 7-8): Beta testing with a pilot group of 100 employees
Phase 4 (Month 9): Company-wide rollout and training
Phase 5 (Months 10-12): Iteration based on feedback and feature enhancements

6. EXPECTED OUTCOMES

- 30% increase in employee satisfaction scores within 12 months
- 20% reduction in stress-related absenteeism
- 15% decrease in healthcare cost growth rate
- 50% employee adoption rate by end of year one
- Improved talent retention, targeting a 10% reduction in voluntary turnover

7. RISKS AND MITIGATION

Risk: Low employee adoption
Mitigation: Executive sponsorship, incentive programs, and targeted communication campaigns

Risk: Data privacy concerns
Mitigation: SOC 2 compliance, anonymized reporting, and transparent data usage policies

Risk: Integration complexity with existing HR systems
Mitigation: Dedicated integration team and phased rollout approach

8. RECOMMENDATION

We recommend approval of this proposal with a budget of $345,000 for the first year. The investment represents approximately 2.8% of annual payroll and is projected to deliver a return on investment of 3.5x within two years through reduced healthcare costs, improved productivity, and decreased turnover. A pilot program with a subset of employees is recommended to validate assumptions before full-scale deployment.
