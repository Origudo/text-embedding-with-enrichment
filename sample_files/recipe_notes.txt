SPICY THAI BASIL CHICKEN (PAD KRAPOW)
Prep time: 10 min | Cook time: 10 min | Servings: 2

Ingredients:
- 300g chicken thighs, minced or finely chopped
- 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 Thai bird's eye chilies, sliced (adjust to taste)
- 1 tablespoon fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 teaspoon oyster sauce
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 1 cup Thai holy basil leaves (or regular basil if unavailable)
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- Steamed jasmine rice for serving
- Fried egg (optional but recommended)

Instructions:
1. Heat oil in a wok or large pan over high heat until smoking.
2. Add garlic and chilies, stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
3. Add minced chicken and spread evenly in the pan. Let it sear without stirring for about 1 minute.
4. Break up the chicken and stir-fry until fully cooked and slightly browned, about 3-4 minutes.
5. Mix fish sauce, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar in a small bowl. Pour over the chicken.
6. Stir-fry for another 1-2 minutes until the sauce coats the meat evenly.
7. Remove from heat and fold in the basil leaves until wilted.
8. Serve over steamed rice. Top with a fried egg with runny yolk if desired.

Tips: The key to authentic Pad Krapow is high heat and not overcrowding the pan. Use a wok if possible. The dish should be slightly dry, not saucy.

CLASSIC ITALIAN TIRAMISU
Prep time: 30 min | Chill time: 4 hours minimum | Servings: 8

Ingredients:
- 6 egg yolks
- 3/4 cup granulated sugar
- 500g mascarpone cheese, room temperature
- 2 cups heavy cream
- 2 cups strong espresso, cooled
- 3 tablespoons coffee liqueur (optional)
- 300g ladyfinger biscuits (savoiardi)
- Cocoa powder for dusting
- Dark chocolate shavings (optional)

Instructions:
1. Whisk egg yolks and sugar together until thick, pale, and doubled in volume (about 5 minutes).
2. Add mascarpone and beat until smooth and combined.
3. In a separate bowl, whip heavy cream to stiff peaks.
4. Gently fold whipped cream into mascarpone mixture until no streaks remain.
5. Combine espresso and coffee liqueur in a shallow dish.
6. Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the espresso mixture (1-2 seconds per side — do not soak).
7. Arrange a layer of dipped ladyfingers in the bottom of a 9x13 dish.
8. Spread half of the cream mixture evenly over the ladyfingers.
9. Repeat with another layer of dipped ladyfingers and remaining cream.
10. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.
11. Before serving, dust generously with cocoa powder and add chocolate shavings.

Tips: Use freshly brewed espresso for best flavor. The longer it chills, the better the texture. Do not oversoak the ladyfingers or the dessert will be soggy.

QUICK WEEKDAY MISO SOUP
Prep time: 5 min | Cook time: 10 min | Servings: 2

Ingredients:
- 4 cups dashi stock (or water with 1 teaspoon dashi granules)
- 3 tablespoons white miso paste
- 100g silken tofu, cubed
- 1 sheet dried seaweed (wakame), rehydrated
- 2 green onions, sliced
- Optional: mushrooms, bok choy, or any vegetables

Instructions:
1. Bring dashi stock to a gentle simmer. Do not boil.
2. Add tofu and any vegetables, simmer for 3 minutes.
3. Remove from heat. Place miso paste in a ladle and dissolve it into the broth.
4. Add rehydrated wakame and green onions.
5. Serve immediately.

Important: Never boil miso paste — it destroys the probiotics and flavor. Always dissolve it after removing from heat.
